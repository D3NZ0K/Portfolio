Context

Ariane, vous me recevez ?

"Kourou Space Coop: Ariane, vous me recevez ?" is a game designed for the CNES (Centre national d'études spatiales). We were tasked with creating a Virtual Reality experience showcasing the key missions of Ariane 5. This initiative followed the success of another game, Orbital Dance, and coincided with the anticipated completion of Ariane 6 between mid-June and mid-July. It was the perfect opportunity to revisit the highlights of Ariane 5's missions.

Pre-production

Concept Art & Blockout

Before diving into full 3D production, I designed concept art for the Mission Control environment and the interactive gloves, then built the blockout to validate space and scale within VR.

Shaders

Cathode Ray Tube & Interaction Shaders

I designed a cathode ray tube shader graph to captivate players' attention, and created a second shader specifically for objects that players can pick up or interact with from a distance — enhancing both visual appeal and gameplay immersion.

Puzzle — Barometer

Pressure Puzzle Barometer

At the request of the Game Designers, I modeled a barometer for a puzzle where the AI reports pressure issues within the Ariane 5 launcher. The player onboard the launcher must adjust the pressure using valves, working collaboratively with the player in Mission Control who monitors the pressure levels via the barometers I designed. Ensuring the barometers were clear and easy to read was critical, as any confusion could hinder gameplay and player understanding.

Puzzle — Fuse Box

Fuse Box & Power Failure Mechanic

As part of the game's new puzzle mechanics, the Game Designers tasked me with modeling a fuse box and individual fuses for a scenario where the AI alerts players to a launcher power failure. To solve this, the player inside the launcher must locate the fuse box and correctly place the new fuses in their designated slots. Each correctly positioned fuse updates a real-time electronic interface in the control base, indicating its status. Once all four fuses are properly installed, the player in the control base validates the sequence, unlocking the booster separation levers and advancing the mission.

UI Design

Menu UI — Design & Integration

I was responsible for the UI design of the game's menus, which included creating backgrounds, buttons, logos, and other visual elements. I started by designing them in Photoshop, focusing on clarity and intuitiveness. Once finalized, I imported and integrated them into Unity, ensuring all resolutions and formats were compatible with the project's requirements.
