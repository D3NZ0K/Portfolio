Gameplay Soutenance — Kintsugi (2025)

My Role

2D Animation & Cinematics

As 2D Animator on this student project, I animated characters using Spine, with a strong focus on acting, emotional expression, and player readability. To accommodate a dense narrative, the project is structured around fragmented biomes, allowing us to showcase the game's universe while maintaining a high level of polish.

I also created cinematics and animated animals and creatures specific to each biome, reinforcing visual variety and narrative depth.

Challenge #1

Cinematics & Collaboration

A major challenge was working on cinematics in collaboration with other team members to set up framing and create the storyboard. This required me to be highly communicative, organized, and clever: I had to create separate animations that seamlessly fit together and play smoothly, while ensuring the player could fully feel the character's emotions, without ever noticing when sequences were cut or combined.

Challenge #2

Spine Rig & Physical Constraints

The character's Spine rig is based on physical constraints to simulate the movement of clothing and hair without the need for manual animation. This approach results in smoother, more realistic motion while optimizing production time.

Challenge #3

Gold Corruption VFX

My third major challenge was designing and animating the gold corruption affecting the character. I worked directly from the character's Spine atlas, drawing and painting over it in Photoshop to define how the corruption would progressively appear.

The main difficulty was finding the right scale and thickness of the gold filaments, as the game features both highly zoomed-in cinematics and heavily zoomed-out gameplay, making visual readability a constant challenge.

Bonus

Particle Systems & Shaders

When I finished my tasks ahead of schedule, I also contributed to creating particle systems and implementing shaders, which allowed me to add extra visual details and enhance the game's immersion.
