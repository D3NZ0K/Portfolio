About me

Game Artist
& Storyteller

Hi! My name is Enzo. I am a 22-year-old Game Art student at IIM in Paris La Défense, and what I like is creating universes, atypical characters, and a strong atmosphere in games.

In my youth I drew my favorite comic book characters or hundreds of Pokémon, and spent evenings playing cooperative games with my uncle. The association of these two passions led me naturally to study Game Art — a job where I want to immerse players in the heart of my imagination.

Being a big volleyball fan, I love teamwork. I'm also very curious, and admittedly, a big talker.

Beyond the screen

Volleyball & Beach Volleyball

Passionate about volleyball since the age of 4, I've never stopped playing. Over the years I've competed at national level, finishing 7th and 12th at the Coupe de France Jeune, and claimed the Île-de-France Beach Volleyball title. Today I still compete at Régional 1 level with Courbevoie.

More than a sport, volleyball has shaped how I work — the communication, discipline and team spirit I've built on the court translate directly into how I collaborate within a game development team.
